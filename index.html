Chronic Absenteeism has reached a record high in New York City Public Schools

by Leonardo Lopez Carreno

As NYC schools enter their final weeks of classes, it has become clear that one of the most important issues for the 2021-2022 academic year was chronic absenteeism. In April, a New York Post report showed that chronic absenteeism (when a student misses 10% of classes in a semester, or more) has risen to 40% this year, a historic high for the city.

While this record high is certainly cause for concern, the issue is far from new. New York City public schools have dealt with rising levels of chronic abscenteeism for several years now, and data shows thatBlack and Latino students have higher rates of chronic abscencies than their Asian and White peers. Additionally, while chronic abscenteeism impacts all New York City schools, some neighborhoods and school districts have found themselves with unusually high rates of absences. The following annotaged image highlights the 5 school districts with the highest percentage of chronically absent students for the 2020-2021 school year. This data visualization shows that the two highest are close together, in the West and Central Harlem and South Bronx neighborhoods. The following three highest districts are close together in Brooklyn, showing how certain areas of the city are particularly vulnerable to this issue.

New York Instructional District 5

District 5, which covers West and Central Harlem, reported a 50.4% of chronically absent students in the 2020-2021 academic year.

New York Instructional District 7

District 7, which covers the South Bronx, reported a 45.4% of chronically absent students in the 2020-2021 academic year.

New York Instructional District 18

District 18, Canarsie and East New York, reported a 41.9% of chronically absent students in the 2020-2021 academic year.

New York School District 17

District 17, which covers Crown Heights, reported a 38.5% of chronically absent students in the 2020-2021 academic year.

New York School District 23

District 23, which covers Ocean Hill and Brownsville, reported a 37.7% of chronically absent students in the 2020-2021 academic year.
